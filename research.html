21cm cosmology at the epoch of reionization

My work thus for in 21cm cosmology has focused on developing the statistical tools needed to relate a characterization of the 21cm power spectrum to physical parameters that govern the formation of the first galaxies and dictate the large scale structure of the universe. In Kern et al. 2017, we build an emulator framework for creating surrogate models of expensive simulations of Cosmic Dawn, and use these to produce a parameter constraint forecast for the HERA experiment, showing it will nominally put strong constraints on reionization parameters, and may help strengthen the constraint on the amplitude of density fluctuations already set by the Planck mission. The code developed for this work is publicly available.
